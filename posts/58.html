Peer Reviews in Software: A Practical Guide

Published Jun. 11, 2025

This book is excellent. It is almost 25 years old, but it should be required reading for every professional working in a programming-related field. I wish that the outlined processes were utilized more often and I firmly believe that they will result in higher quality programs with far fewer bugs or defects.

In short, the book is a comprehensive guide to peer reviews in software development, covering the process, benefits, and best practices. It is grounded in experience and makes many references to studies and other texts which support its claims.

The first two chapters strongly focus on selling the process to management by extolling its benefits, while the rest of the book focuses on the process itself. Much time is spent explaining the different types of reviews and how to perform them, with detailed instructions and examples for each step.

Based on his personal website, Karl Wiegers has published a number of other books which I am looking forward to reading through.
